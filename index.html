Braille is a notational system developed in the early 1800s. Notational systems represent the sounds or words of a language. In the case of braille, the notational system is tactile instead of visual.

Each letter of the alphabet is encoded in a single braille cell. In English Braille, this cell is arranged using six dots in a 2 by 3 matrix.
